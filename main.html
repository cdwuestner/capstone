About

Crazy Castles is a real-time strategy HTML5 game developed by Bridgette Smith and Chris Wuestner (Team Grus) as a final project for CS 467: Online Capstone Project. We primarily used the melonJS game engine to develop this game. We also used Tiled to create our gameboard and Texture Packer to create animated bases.

Rules and How To Play

  • At the main menu, press the [e] key to begin a game at "Easy" difficulty or the [h] key to begin a game at "Hard" difficulty. Enemy health capacity (Skeletons and Sorcerers) is doubled on "Hard" difficulty.
  • You begin the game with three units already built: one Warrior, one Wizard, and one Healer. A flickering effect is applied to whichever unit is currently selected. Press the Spacebar to change which unit is selected.
  • Your selected unit will move to whichever location on the gameboard you click
  • Melee battles will occur whenever your units come into contact with enemy units. Both the player unit and enemy unit will take a random amount of damage based on the attack power of the opposing unit.
  • If a unit is defeated (its health falls to 0), it is removed from the gameboard.
  • Wizard units can use magic to shoot projectiles in four directions that will damage enemies that they hit. A selected Wizard can shoot magic with the arrow keys (the up key shoots upwards, right key shoots right, etc.). Wizards can inflict a small amount of melee damage as well.
  • Healer units can use magic to shoot projectiles in four directions that will heal player units that they hit. Like wizard magic, a selected Healer's magic is used with the arrow keys. Healers cannot inflict any melee damage.
  • There are two types of enemy units: Skeletons and Sorcerers. Skeletons have higher maximum health, while Sorcerers are faster and have higher melee attack power.
  • Every 20 seconds, you gain another "stored unit". This means that you can add another of your units to the gameboard. Press [w] to add a wizard, [e] to add a Healer, and [r] to add a Warrior. The number of units you can build (stored units) is displayed above the gameboard.
  • Every 20 seconds, another enemy unit (either a Skeleton or Sorcerer) is added to the gameboard.
  • Each game, there are three randomly-placed bases in the center of the gameboard. A base will turn blue if it is currently occupied by the player, and red if it is occupied by the enemy. Each base held provides +25 health capacity to the entire team holding it.
  • The goal of the game is to defeat the enemy's Boss, which occupies their castle, before the enemy is able to defeat the Princess within your castle.
  • Both the Boss and Princess have very high maximum health and melee attack power, but remain stationary within their respective castles.
  • You may save your game and pause at any time by pressing [p].
